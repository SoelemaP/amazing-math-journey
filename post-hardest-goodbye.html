Saying Goodbye: The hardest thing I have ever done💔

As I embarked on my journey to Italy, I felt a swirl of emotions—excitement for the new adventure ahead, but also a deep, aching sadness. Leaving home for the very first time was harder than I could have imagined, especially saying goodbye to my mom. We’ve always been incredibly close—some would call me a mama’s girl, and they wouldn’t be wrong. She’s been my rock, my biggest supporter, from the day I was born. The longest we’d ever been apart was a three-week vacation to Curaçao when I was twelve.

Thankfully, with Wi-Fi on my KLM flights and at the airports, we were able to stay connected throughout my long journey. Every message, every call brought me a little bit of comfort. But the real challenge came after leaving Lamezia Terme Airport. When I arrived on campus, exhausted and overwhelmed, the Wi-Fi just wouldn’t work. I couldn’t contact my mom to let her know I’d made it safely. It was already late, and I couldn’t go to the store to buy a local SIM card. For a moment, I even thought about walking to the store in the dark, just to hear her voice. But common sense kicked in—it wasn’t safe, and I turned back toward my apartment, feeling defeated.

That first night was one of the hardest nights of my life. Alone in a new country, with no way to reach my mom, I felt completely cut off from the one person I needed most. I didn’t just feel homesick—I felt heartbroken. I’d never felt so far away from home, and I honestly didn’t know how I was going to make it through the night. If someone had offered me a ticket back home right then, I would’ve taken it without a second thought.

But life has a way of surprising you when you least expect it. On my way back to my apartment, I met a kind girl from Cuba who offered me her hotspot. I can’t tell you the relief I felt just being able to send a simple WhatsApp voice message to my mom: “I made it. I’m here. There’s an issue with the Wi-Fi, but I’m okay. I will contact you tomorrow.” My voice was shaky, and I fought back tears as I sent it. It wasn’t the conversation I wanted, but it was enough to ease the anxiety gnawing at me.

Once I got back to my room, I couldn’t hold it in anymore. I sat on my bed and cried; the homesickness came crashing over me like a wave. For a moment, I doubted everything. Could I really do this? Would the next two years be filled with moments like this—isolated, overwhelmed, and missing home so much it hurt? I wasn’t sure I could make it through. I even cried myself to sleep.

The next day, things started looking up. I went to the welcome office to fix the Wi-Fi issue, and then I bought a SIM card so I could finally call my mom. The moment I heard her voice, I almost broke down again. We talked for what felt like forever—so much so that I got on the wrong bus on my way back to campus. But none of that mattered. Hearing her voice reminded me that no matter how far I go, I’m never truly alone.

That second day was a turning point for me. After speaking to her, I felt like I could finally breathe, and for the first time, I started to truly appreciate where I was and more importantly, what I was about to do. Pursuing a master's degree in Italy, was now my reality. And although being away from home would never be easy, I realized that the journey I was on was bigger than my fear, bigger than my homesickness.

This experience taught me something I’ll carry with me forever: no matter how old you get, you’re never too old to miss your mom. Leaving home is hard, especially when an entire ocean separates you. But it’s the love we leave behind that gives us the strength to move forward. Here’s to embracing the adventure ahead while cherishing the people who anchor us to who we are. 💖🌟

Embrace every adventure, because each step away from home teaches you more about yourself and deepens the love for those you miss the most.
Arrivederci! 👋🏾
