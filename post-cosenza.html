"Cosenza, Che Bella!" (Cosenza, How Beautiful!)

Ciao, amici! 🇮🇹✨
I took a trip to the beautiful city of Cosenza, and let me just say – che bella! 🌞 Just a quick 10-minute bus ride from the Università della Calabria campus in Rende (that’s where I am!), and we were there. Fun fact: Cosenza is both a province and a city here in the Calabria region of southern Italy.

One of my first stops was Supermercati 5 Continenti, which literally means “Supermarket of the 5 Continents.” 🌍🛒 The name fits because this store is a treasure trove of global flavors and products! I found boulanger, Chinese tayer, plantain, and even some familiar snacks, like a little slice of Suriname in Italy. There’s something magical about finding bits of home when you’re so far away. ❤️

After soaking up that slice of home, we headed to what I call “Cosenza’s city center” (actually called “Corso Mazzini “). It’s a vibrant, lively street lined with incredible statues, fashion stores, and stunning old buildings. You can shop till you drop with brands like Zara, Mango, H&M, and OVS right at your fingertips. 🛍️👗 Even the window-shopping game is on another level here!

Thanks to a kind Cuban friend who showed me around, I even explored the Centro Storico, Cosenza’s Old Town! This area, known as one of the oldest in southern Italy, was once the capital of the Bruzi people (an ancient Italic tribe from Calabria, were known for their rich culture and resistance against Roman expansion before being absorbed into the Roman Empire). Its narrow, winding streets and historical buildings tell stories of a rich past, and every corner is packed with hidden gems. 🏰 Walking through it literally felt like walking through the heart of old Cosenza (I guess that’s why it’s called that)!

As I explored, I also visited some breathtaking churches, including the Chiesa di Santa Teresa del Bambino Gesù (Church of Saint Teresa of the Child Jesus), a peaceful sanctuary. There was also the Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta, a beautiful piece of history, and finally, the Chiesa di San Francesco di Paola, honoring Calabria's own patron saint. ⛪✨

Of course, I couldn’t leave without tasting some authentic Italian gelato. 🍦 Let’s just say, I have been forever changed. Gelato has officially ruined regular ice cream for me! Delizioso!

For more about my Italian adventures, and photos, head to my Instagram! 📸✨

Remember, every journey is filled with unexpected surprises and new friends – it’s all about embracing each moment and making memories that last. 💖
Arrivederci, amici! 🌍💫
